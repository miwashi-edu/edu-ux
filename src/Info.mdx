# UX Components in Web Development

A categorized reference list of common UX components used in web development, each with a brief description.

## üìÑ Page Layout

### [Header](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-header--docs)
Topmost section, usually holds logo, nav, and user access. Often includes branding and quick access to key actions. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-header--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Anchor user orientation and brand identity
- Provide immediate access to navigation and key actions

**When to Use:**
- On every page for consistency
- To house navigation, search, and user profile access

**UX Risks:**
- Overcrowding with too many actions or links
- Insufficient contrast or unclear boundaries

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses flexbox/grid for layout, sticky positioning, and shadow for separation

---

### [Footer](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-footer--docs)
Bottom area for copyright, contact, and secondary links. Good for legal info, links, and site-wide actions. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-footer--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Provide closure and access to secondary information
- Reduce cognitive load by grouping less critical links

**When to Use:**
- For legal, contact, or site-wide links
- To reinforce brand and provide help resources

**UX Risks:**
- Overloading with too much content
- Low contrast or small text

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses padding, background color, and border-top for separation

---

### [Logo](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-logo--docs)
Vertical area for nav or tools, left or right aligned. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-logo--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Instantly recognizable brand anchor
- Aids in user orientation

**When to Use:**
- In header, sidebar, or splash screens

**UX Risks:**
- Too large or too small disrupts balance
- Unclear or low-contrast logos harm recognition

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses fixed sizing, margin, and alignment utilities

---

### [Main](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-main--docs)
The primary content area of a page. Should be unique per page for accessibility. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-main--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Focus user attention on core content
- Support accessibility and screen reader navigation

**When to Use:**
- As the main content wrapper on every page

**UX Risks:**
- Multiple main elements confuse assistive tech
- Insufficient spacing reduces readability

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses margin, padding, and max-width for readability

---

### [Container](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-container--docs)
A fixed-width wrapper that centers page content. Useful for consistent layouts. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-container--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Maintain consistent content width for readability
- Center content for balanced layouts

**When to Use:**
- For main content, forms, or cards

**UX Risks:**
- Too wide or too narrow harms scanability

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses max-width, margin auto, and responsive breakpoints

---

### [Grid](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-grid--docs)
Structural layout of rows and columns. Enables responsive design. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-grid--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Organize content for easy scanning
- Support responsive, adaptable layouts

**When to Use:**
- For dashboards, galleries, or multi-column layouts

**UX Risks:**
- Overly complex grids can confuse users
- Poor spacing reduces clarity

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses CSS grid/flexbox, gap, and media queries

---

### [Section](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-section--docs)
Thematic division of content. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-section--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Break content into digestible, thematic blocks
- Aid in visual hierarchy and flow

**When to Use:**
- For separating topics, features, or steps

**UX Risks:**
- Too many sections can fragment the experience

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses padding, margin, and background color for separation

---

### [Hero](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-hero--docs)
Large top area often with image and call-to-action. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-hero--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Capture attention and communicate primary value
- Guide users to the main action

**When to Use:**
- On landing pages or major sections

**UX Risks:**
- Overly large or busy heroes can distract from content

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses large typography, background images, and flex alignment

---

### [Card](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-card--docs)
Boxed container for grouping related content or actions. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/page-layout-card--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Group related information for easy scanning
- Provide clear boundaries for actions or content

**When to Use:**
- For product, profile, or info summaries

**UX Risks:**
- Too many cards can overwhelm
- Inconsistent card sizes break flow

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses border-radius, shadow, and spacing

---

Stays fixed while user scrolls. [See Storybook](http://localhost:6006/?path=/docs/experience-enhancers-stickyelement--docs)

**UX Goals:**
- Keep key actions or info visible
- Support quick access

**When to Use:**
- For nav, actions, or summaries

**UX Risks:**
- Too many sticky elements clutter UI
- Can obscure content

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses position: sticky, z-index, and spacing

- Enable seamless content discovery
- Reduce friction in browsing

- Hard to reach footer or actions

**CSS Notes:**
- Uses scroll event, loading indicator, and spacing
